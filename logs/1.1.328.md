# Roman Society 1.1.328

Release date: 2026-06-16

A full-mod audit pass: a navigation fix, a self-targeting realism fix, a type
robustness fix, and a requested redesign that splits criminal and political
espionage into two distinct systems.

## Espionage Redesign: Criminal Informers vs Political Agents

Previously the crime menu's "Build spy network" opened the exact same intrigue
menu as Roman power systems, so there was really only one spy network doing two
unrelated jobs. These are now two separate systems with separate abilities, as
requested.

- **Criminal informers (delatores)** - hired from the crime menu ("Hire
  delatores"). Stored in the new `society.crimeInformers`, keyed by house.
  - Raise the chance that a watched house's crimes are detected.
  - Deepen your audits/denunciations against that house (more members are combed)
    and reduce the calumnia penalty of a baseless denunciation.
  - Add to a house's visible suspicion score.
  - Cost is cash-driven (paying informers), scaling per level up to 5.
- **Political agents** - planted from Roman power systems > Political intrigue.
  Stored in `society.spyNetworks` as before, but they no longer touch crime
  detection at all. Their job is purely political: uncover secrets, create
  blackmail hooks, coerce coalitions/factions, and run conspiracies.
- Crime detection (`crimeDetectionChance`) now reads criminal informers per
  house instead of political spy networks per character, so the two systems no
  longer share an effect.
- New state `society.crimeInformers` is initialized in the data-shape pass and
  pruned for houses that no longer exist.

## Navigation Fix

- Fixed the Back button in the imperial-pardon screen (`seekImperialPardon`).
  It was dispatched through `politicsAction` with the `openCrimes` action, but
  `openCrimes` is not in that whitelist, so Back fell through to the politics
  hub instead of returning to the crime menu. It now routes through
  `romanSystemsAction` like the rest of the crime menu.
- Re-audited every `politicsAction`/`romanSystemsAction` Back target against its
  dispatcher whitelist and confirmed every other button resolves correctly.

## Realism / Self-Targeting Fix

- The "Imperial justice (investigate, judge)" action no longer appears on the
  player's own character. An Emperor could previously open imperial cognitio on
  himself (investigate/imprison/exile/execute self), which is absurd. Judging
  family is still allowed (historically real); only self-targeting is blocked.
  A matching guard was added inside `openImperialActions` for safety.

## Robustness

- `enslavedPurchaseInfo` now compares dynasty ids with string coercion when
  deciding whether a character is "already household", matching the rest of the
  function and preventing a type mismatch from ever re-offering an owned slave
  for purchase.
- The criminal-informer house list uses `politicsRivalHouses` (which excludes the
  player house by id and flag) so you can never target your own house.

## Audit Coverage (verified clean)

- No mojibake / double-encoded text anywhere in the codebase.
- No duplicate method definitions within a module, and every cross-module method
  override correctly wraps the previous definition via the `previous*` pattern.
- Every button `method`/`action` reference resolves to a real method.
- Dynasty-id equality checks elsewhere compare values from the same state source,
  so they are type-consistent.

## Private Loans Act Like a Real Lender

A prudent lender never ties up more than half of its liquid capital in a single
private loan, and never lends money it does not have. This now applies uniformly
to the player and to NPC houses.

- `privateLoanOfferAmounts` caps any single offer at 50% of the lender's liquid
  capital (previously up to 100% for the player). This covers the player offering
  credit, the NPC-to-NPC loan simulation, and the helper used elsewhere.
- The NPC-to-NPC simulation no longer pre-scales the lender's cash by 0.55 before
  calling the shared helper (which would have double-capped); it passes the full
  cash and lets the 50% rule apply once.
- `privateLoanRequestAmounts` (a house lending to the player) caps at 50% of the
  house's cash (was 55%).
- Execution guards in `offerPrivateLoan` and `requestPrivateLoan` reject any
  amount above 50% of the lender's cash, so a stale UI can never push a loan past
  the rule.
- Institutional Bank of Rome loans are unchanged: they are credit from the
  institution, not from the player's capital, and remain repayable over time.

## Realistic Crime Ecosystem (theft, murder, security)

Crime is now a connected ecosystem rather than a set of isolated per-character
actions.

- **Theft is now a house-targeted crime.** "Steal from household" no longer
  appears on every character. Instead, "Theft and burglary" lives in the crime
  menu: pick a house to rob, choose an approach, and detection is handled by the
  crime system as *furtum*. Bigger, more powerful houses are harder and riskier;
  getting caught can mean a furtum charge, not just relation loss. The per-character
  "Steal from household" action is removed, and `vanillaCharacterActions` now also
  filters out any legacy per-character steal action that may be persisted on a
  character or cached from an older build, so the only bundled per-character
  actions are Play As and Attempt Murder.
- **Murder is tied to the crime system with detection by importance and
  security.** The per-character "Attempt Murder" action stays, but its bandit
  attempt now (a) is harder against important and well-guarded targets, and (b)
  registers the attempt as a Society crime (*sicarii*, a Lex Cornelia capital
  crime) with a detection chance. If the delatores trace it back to you, you face
  exposure, a trial, and the victim's house turns on you.
- **Bodyguards.** From the player's own Society sheet there is now a "Security &
  bodyguards" screen. Hiring bodyguards (with monthly upkeep) lowers the chance an
  assassination against you succeeds and raises the chance plots against you are
  uncovered as crimes. Emperors are additionally guarded.
- **NPCs assassinate each other and the player.** A monthly simulation lets
  hostile houses send *sicarii* against rivals or against the player. Success
  depends on the target's security (your bodyguards protect you) and importance;
  attempts can be detected as crimes, exposing the plotter's house to trial and
  reputation loss. Mod-generated characters are real game characters, so kills
  resolve through the base-game `daapi.kill`.
- New crime types `sicarii` and `furtum` were added to the crime catalog, and new
  Society state (`security`, `crimeInformers`, `assassinationLog`) is initialized
  and pruned.
