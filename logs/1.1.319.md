# Roman Society 1.1.319

## 1.1.319 - Phases 2-4: Land feuds, abduction, and Senate -> Dictator -> Emperor

New self-contained module `modules/politics.js` (mixin `_mixinCorSocietyPolitics`),
opened from a new hub entry **"Senate & Politics"**. All of it has REAL gameplay
effects (cash/influence/prestige, house wealth/power/relation/heat, world state),
not just labels.

### Phase 2 — Land feuds & seizure

- **Press a land claim:** seize property value from a rival house (transfers wealth/
  cash to you), raises rivalry/heat. Success scales with relative power, your
  household stewardship, and your political rank.
- **Raid / blockade:** temporary revenue debuff on a rival's estates (reuses
  `house.ai.modifiers`).
- **Fortify:** protect your own estates for a while.
- **AI:** rival houses seize property from each other monthly ("House X seizes the
  horrea on the Aventine from House Y").

### Phase 3 — Abduction for extortion

- **Abduct a rival's relative:** risk of exposure (scandal). On success you hold the
  captive; then **ransom** (cash from their house), **demand a binding favour (hook)**,
  or **release** for goodwill. Their house may attempt a **rescue**; captives auto-free
  at a deadline.

### Phase 4 — Senate -> Dictator -> Emperor

- **Build a faction**, accrue **auctoritas** (prestige/influence/faction/offices/
  property), climb toward power.
- **Seek the Dictatorship** (republic only): gated (senatorial + faction >= 3 +
  auctoritas >= 70), a Senate vote that can fail. Dictatorship is **time-limited** —
  the Senate forces a lay-down when the term ends.
- **Lay down the dictatorship** (Cincinnatus) for honour, or **Make yourself
  Emperor** — a hard, multi-stage bid that **frequently fails** (failed vote, exposed
  plot, optimates backlash) with costly branches: forced abdication, **proscription**,
  or **civil war**. The first Emperor **must** have been Dictator first.
- **Empire:** succession is by designation/adoption but **contestable** — rival houses
  build pressure to switch it to "by wealth" or "by power"; **defend the succession**
  or lose ground. **Imperial powers:** appoint provincial governors, **declare war**
  (war-time effects across all houses), and **hold games/donatives**.

### Real advantages of supreme office (not just a title)

- **Monthly upkeep:** Emperor gains +90 cash / +30 influence / +8 prestige and his
  house gains power/wealth/stability each month; Dictator gains +45 / +25 / +4 and
  house power/wealth. (Emperor > Dictator.)
- **Power bonus** improves your feud and faction-recruitment success while you hold
  supreme office.

### The Imperator symbol

- New asset `assets/aquila.svg` — the eagle of Rome with **SPQR**. The title
  **"Imperator"** and the eagle now accompany imperial menus, the emperor
  announcement, and the politics screen when you rule.

### Plumbing

- `modules/politics` added to the load manifest (`monthly.js`) and the mixin list
  (`engine.js`); `simulatePoliticsMonthly` runs each month.
- Fixed: the bundled `cor_translator` was missing from the load manifest — now added,
  so the bundled translator actually loads.
- Runtime version aligned to `1.1.319`.

### Notes / Not Confirmed

- Numbers (gates, chances, income) are first-pass balance; not playtested in-game.
- Designed to degrade safely if base-game fields are absent. Please report behaviour
  so the balance and the Senate-office detection can be tuned.
