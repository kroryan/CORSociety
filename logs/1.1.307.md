# Roman Society 1.1.307

This is the first entry in the new structured changelog system.

The old root README mixed feature documentation, design notes, and release notes. Correct historical notes from that file are preserved below as recovered history, but older exact version boundaries are not reconstructed unless they were present in the files.

## 1.1.307 - Dynasty And Tree Integrity Pass

### Fixed

- Cadet branches in the player's dynasty are no longer automatically pulled back into the player's origin house just because they share the vanilla dynasty id.
- House resolution now respects a valid explicit `corSocietyHouseId` first, then inherited parental branch membership, then the current player house, then the dynasty origin house.
- New children of a cadet branch can inherit the cadet house through their parents before falling back to the player origin house.
- `syncPlayerHouseRecord()` skips members already resolved to a valid non-player cadet house, preventing branch reabsorption during monthly sync.
- NPC inter-house marriages now determine mother and father houses from the actual candidate source house instead of comparing `character.dynastyId` to `house.id`, which failed for cadet houses.
- Marriage compatibility now rejects close blood relatives through known parent and ancestor links.
- The marriage validator reports close-kin marriages in debug snapshots.
- Retiring a house now immediately removes it from active dynasty `houseIds` and clears invalid dynasty origin/head references before final maintenance.
- The dynasty validator now warns when a dynasty has an invalid head house.
- Generated child relatives created for a house head without a spouse now use a dead ghost co-parent instead of accidentally using the head's own missing parent as the child's other parent.
- Generated parent/ancestor death causes now reflect plausible age at death instead of always using `old age`.
- Parent `childrenIds` are updated in local state immediately after generated children are linked, reducing stale in-memory relationship data during the same tick.

### Changed

- The old versioned dynasty-house repair remains versioned. It is treated as a migration pass.
- A separate lightweight dynasty-house maintenance pass now runs during startup/monthly flows and can be forced after house creation or retirement.
- Final startup and monthly maintenance can repair a bounded number of cadet branches so branch membership is not dependent on opening a tree.
- `sameCharacterId()` moved to core startup utilities.
- `houseIdForCharacter()` moved to the dynasty model, because it creates or resolves house records and should not depend on visual overlay modules.
- The runtime version is aligned to `1.1.307` so versioned migrations run for this schema pass.

### Preserved By Design

- Roman Society still fabricates dead ancestors, grandparents, collateral ancestors, and genealogy connectors.
- Tree preparation may still create bounded dead connectors where the model needs historical roots.
- This version improves when and how those ancestors are created; it does not remove ancestor fabrication.

## Correct Historical Notes Recovered From The Old README

The following notes were present in the previous README and remain correct at a system level.

### Actions And UI

- Adds current-character actions for Roman Society, House Shield, Family Wardrobe, Bank of Rome, and Household Slaves.
- Adds Society close/back navigation for modal flows.
- Bundles compatible standalone actions: Play As, Attempt Murder, animal stealing, Disinherit, Restore Inheritance, Bank of Rome, Coemptio, Household Slaves, and optional developer tools.
- Lets Society surface compatible vanilla/other-mod character actions from character pages when the game exposes them.
- Uses copied vanilla interface icons where the mod API allows local assets.
- Shows visual relationship badges, house crest labels, and character status icons in Society lists.

### Dynasties, Houses, And Trees

- Groups houses into social orders using existing game data: dynasty prestige, heritage, jobs, inheritance, Senate links, and living members.
- Separates dynasties from houses: every active dynasty has an origin/successor house, may gain cadet houses, and tracks headship independently.
- Strong cadet houses can challenge and replace the current dynasty head without renaming houses.
- Cadet houses carry parent/founder/branch metadata and move the founder's descendant branch.
- Dynasty tree and house tree share the same underlying genealogy with strict scopes.
- Full dynasty tree resolves the selected dynasty and its houses.
- House tree filters that graph down to the selected house branch.
- Tree portrait cards show Society house crest and origin/cadet house labels.
- External spouses may appear visually, but they are not tree roots, tree seeds, or expandable house members.
- Slaves, captured dependants, and unrelated houses are kept out of free dynasty and house-tree expansion.
- Tree rendering uses connected components, canonical couple roots, duplicate suppression, and per-month runtime caching.
- House trees build from the selected house, with the selected character as focus rather than the only source.
- House trees preserve real connected uncles, cousins, siblings, descendants, and collateral branches.
- Dead houses move to a Past Affairs archive and keep historical family tree data where characters still exist.

### Generated People And Ancestors

- Generated houses are added when social orders have too few living non-household characters.
- Generated characters are real game characters created through `daapi.generateCharacter`.
- Generated characters receive real ids, vanilla Roman looks, vanilla traits, `flagDoNotCull`, and relationship links where appropriate.
- Generated houses prefer young adult founders and try to keep at least one living man and one living woman.
- Generated children receive parent links through existing spouses or generated dead connectors.
- Missing generated parent pairs can be repaired later so trees remain coherent.
- Portraits use vanilla Citizen of Rome assets resolved from `character.look`.
- Wardrobe stores lightweight Society outfit choices and repairs unsafe older custom looks back to vanilla-compatible data.

### Marriage, Romance, And Family Life

- Arranged marriages and AI house marriages call `daapi.performMarriage`.
- If the vanilla marriage API rejects a wedding, Society does not fake spouse links.
- Society marriages affect prestige, influence, relations, support, and costs according to social order.
- NPC house pregnancies use `daapi.impregnate`; the base game resolves births.
- Courtship and lover relationships are stored in Society state instead of vanilla `spouseId`.
- Exposed affairs can cause scandal, divorce fallout, relation damage, and paternity correction.
- Lover pregnancies track hidden biological paternity and can later transfer child dynasty when revealed.
- Family care events support sick, stressed, depressed, wounded, or otherwise vulnerable household members.
- Child tutorship offers and family invitations create lightweight recurring social decisions.

### Slavery And Freedmen

- The visible slave order uses the internal `poor` key for save compatibility.
- Slave houses, market candidates, captured dependants, owned slaves, and manumitted freedmen are represented by real characters where possible.
- Slaves can have origins, owner links, type profiles, work roles, sale/manumission flow, savings, and freedom goals.
- Owned household slaves can perform modest capped tasks for accounts, education, treatment, entertainment, security, or labor.
- Slave marriages are restricted to owned slaves of different gender.
- Household slave couples can have children through the game's pregnancy API.
- Private company can create illegitimate slave-born children with hidden paternity.
- Eligible slave-born children can later be manumitted or legitimized.
- Manumitted slaves become freed citizens/liberti in their own Freedmen house.
- Freedmen houses may try to buy and free enslaved close relatives.
- False slave markers on legitimate player-family members are repaired conservatively.

### Banking, Economy, And Politics

- NPC houses are simulated as virtual players with cash, influence, prestige, property, focus, and AI state.
- Houses pursue agendas such as office, wealth, honor, marriage, security, and revenge.
- Houses can rise or fall between social orders as simulated wealth, power, and stability change.
- House economy uses vanilla Citizen of Rome property keys, values, revenue formulas, stewardship limits, sale rates, economy-of-scale behavior, and senatorial restrictions.
- Bank of Rome supports player bank loans, private loans, player lending, player borrowing, NPC-to-NPC lending, repayment, extension, default, and debt-bond consequences.
- Trade compacts, patronage, favors, rivals, allies, scandals, petitions, slander, inheritance disputes, and office campaigns affect the social map.
- Trade compacts do not stack infinitely and can break when relations collapse or rivalry starts.
- Changes in the player's cash, influence, or prestige can shift Society relations.

### Compatibility And Stability

- Roman Society does not use `setCurrentCharacter` for Society browsing.
- Vanilla family screens are opened by selecting a viewed character, not by taking control away from the player.
- Extended kin visibility is capped and recalculated monthly to preserve enough family context while avoiding Android stalls.
- Large icon assets are loaded lazily where possible.
- The achievement compatibility hook is intentional and should not be removed.
