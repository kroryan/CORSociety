# Roman Society 1.1.320

## 1.1.320 - Real property feuds, choosable abduction, laws, hints, civil war

Big expansion of the Senate & Politics module (`modules/politics.js`).

### Property is now real and transferable

- NPC houses already own real vanilla property (`house.ai.propertyDetails`: farmland,
  vinyard, orchard, prime*, latifundia, insulae, boats, animals), seeded and bought by
  AI — the same types the player can own.
- **Land claims now seize a SPECIFIC property you choose.** Win the claim, then pick
  which real holding to take (or rustle animals); it is removed from the rival and
  added to your house. Falls back to extorting coin if they have no estates.
- **Abduction lets you choose the victim** from the selected house's actual tree
  members (never random). Ransom can **demand a specific property** (transferred to
  your house) as well as cash, a binding favour, or release.
- All these actions show their **cost (cash / influence / prestige)** on the button
  with vanilla-style icons, applied once (no double charging).

### Laws & policy (not just for the throne)

- As **Dictator** you can propose **power laws** (Lex de Imperio, pack the Senate,
  legibus scribundis, Dictator perpetuo, march on Rome) that build *legislative
  progress* toward Emperor and improve the odds of the bid.
- **Policy laws** (Dictator or Emperor) with real winners and losers, e.g. tax the
  oil latifundia and subsidise herders, **Lex Agraria**, **Lex Claudia**, **Lex
  Frumentaria** (grain dole), **Novae Tabulae** (debt cancellation), harbour dues —
  they apply real revenue modifiers to affected houses by what they own, and shift
  those houses' relations toward or against you.

### Interactive path + hints

- The politics screen now shows a **"Next step" hint** telling you exactly what you
  still need to become Dictator / Emperor.
- The path is interactive: build faction -> seek Dictatorship -> pass laws to raise
  legislative progress -> bid for the purple, or force it via Dictator perpetuo /
  marching on Rome (civil war).

### Civil war can kill you — you continue as your heir

- Bold laws can ignite **civil war**. Win it and you become Imperator; **lose and
  there is a real chance your character is executed** (`daapi.kill`), after which the
  base game continues your play as your heir. Otherwise you are driven from power and
  proscribed.

### Access gating

- **Senate & Politics is locked until the Equestrian order (equites).** The hub entry
  is shown disabled with a tooltip until you qualify; opening it early shows a locked
  notice.
- The hub entry now sits **above Bank of Rome**.

### Notes

- Numbers are first-pass balance, not playtested. Use `debugMakeDictator` /
  `debugMakeEmperor` (see README) to test the upper tiers.
- Runtime version aligned to `1.1.320`.
