# Roman Society 1.1.324

Release date: 2026-06-16

This release fixes the **real** root cause of the slave-disappearing bug that 1.1.323
only partially addressed. The problem was never in the purchase code itself — it was in
two state-maintenance passes that run on almost every `ensure()` and every monthly tick.

## Slave Bug — Real Root Cause

Bought slaves are deliberately placed in the **player's own dynasty** so the
owner relationship works. Two repair/sync passes wrongly interpreted "shares the
player's dynasty" as proof that the character was free family, and quietly
un-enslaved every purchased slave:

- **`repairFalsePlayerSlaveFlags`** (runs on most `ensure()` calls and on month change):
  it treats any player-dynasty member as "protected family", and its owned-slave guard
  was `ownedSlaveIds[id] && !protectedFamily`. Because a bought slave is in the player's
  dynasty, `protectedFamily` was true, so the guard failed, the function stripped every
  slave flag and **removed the slave from `society.playerSlaves`**, turning it into a
  free family member. Since `openManageSlave` calls `ensure()` immediately after a
  purchase, the slave vanished the instant you pressed Back — exactly the reported
  "I buy it, press Back, and it isn't bought" behaviour, plus the "disappears on the
  next turn" behaviour.
  - Fix: a character is now recognised as a genuinely owned slave when it is tracked in
    `society.playerSlaves` with enslaved markers, owned by another house, or a slave
    bastard — regardless of whether it shares the player's dynasty. Those are never
    "repaired".

- **`syncPlayerHouseRecord`** (also runs on `ensure()`/monthly): it only classified a
  character as a slave when it was `explicitSlave && slaveBastard`, so a normal bought
  slave fell into the free-member list and then had its `enslaved_dependant` origin
  stripped.
  - Fix: explicitly enslaved characters that are tracked in `society.playerSlaves`, are
    enslaved dependants, or are slave bastards are now classified into `slaveIds` and
    keep their markers.

- `buyEnslavedCharacter` now also stamps `corSocietyOrigin: 'enslaved_dependant'` so all
  three acquisition paths (market offer, known enslaved candidate, generated) mark slaves
  identically.

Net effect: purchased slaves stay bought, survive turns, and you can own several at once.
The two market buy paths (`buySlave` for generated offers, `buyEnslavedCharacter` for
known enslaved people) both work and both feed the same `playerSlaves` list.

## Carried Over From 1.1.323

This build also contains the 1.1.323 work: the earlier `buySlave` hardening
(charge only after a valid slave is secured, clear the market flag, refund on failure),
the bundled-mod delegation audit, the Imperator/Dictator standing label, the spouse
"Lie together" route, and the Optimates/Populares faction layer with law-vote bias.
