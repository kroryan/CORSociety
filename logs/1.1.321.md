# Roman Society 1.1.321

## 1.1.321 - Dictator mandates, Senate voting, and imperial justice

A more historically grounded, interactive political system.

### Different dictator mandates (Roman causae)

- Seeking the dictatorship now asks **for what cause**, with real Republican
  mandates of different terms and character:
  - **Rei gerundae causa** (wage a war), **Seditionis sedandae causa** (crush
    sedition), **Comitiorum habendorum causa** (hold the elections) — limited,
    honourable, dead ends for ambition.
  - **Legibus scribundis et rei publicae constituendae** (Sulla) — a long
    **legislative** mandate. Only this mandate lets you pass power laws and make the
    bid for the purple. (magister equitum / master of horse referenced.)
- The dictatorship is still time-limited and forces a Cincinnatus-style lay-down.

### Senators propose, the Senate votes; Dictators decree; Emperors edict

- A plain **senator** can now open the laws menu but may only **propose** laws —
  the **Senate then votes** (chance based on auctoritas); failure costs face.
- A **Dictator** (with the legislative mandate) **enacts power laws by decree**.
- An **Emperor** enacts policy by **edict**.
- Equestrians (politics unlocked) who are not yet senators cannot legislate.

### Richer gate + subtle, inviting hints

- The dictatorship now also requires real **means** (wealth in cash/estates or great
  influence), phrased as in-world guidance.
- The "Next step" hint on the politics screen tells you, invitingly, exactly what you
  still lack.

### Imperial justice — per-character emperor actions

- When you are **Emperor**, every character's Society sheet gains **"Imperial
  justice"**. Order a **cognitio (inquiry)**; suspicion is drawn from the house's
  heat/rivalry and the character's traits. If a crime is found — *repetundae*
  (extortion), *adulterium*, sedition, conspiracy, **maiestas (treason)** — you may,
  according to the crime: **imprison**, **confiscate property (proscription)**,
  **banish into exile**, **execute for treason** (`daapi.kill`), or **pardon**
  (clemency). You can also **demand tribute** for the imperial fisc or **grant
  imperial favour** (patronage binds a house to you).

### Notes

- All actions cost prestige/influence/cash as appropriate, shown on the buttons with
  vanilla-style icons, applied once.
- First-pass balance, not playtested. Use `debugMakeEmperor` to try imperial justice.
- Runtime version aligned to `1.1.321`.
